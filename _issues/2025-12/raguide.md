The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess is a game that needs no introduction, and if you're here, you've most likely completed the [main set](https://retroachievements.org/game2/3934). This set was developed by the king of challenge sets: [AlexHylian](https://retroachievements.org/user/AlexHylian). While other games in the series have extremely popular and thriving speedrunning scenes, Twilight Princess does not get as much attention as its counterparts. As such, it is likely that many reading this are unfamiliar with the glitches and tricks that this game has to offer--I certainly was--and this makes the set feel pretty intimidating. The goal of this guide will be to provide a general route and resources to help you get one of the rarest Zelda masteries on RA. Good luck!

## General Info
### Introduction

There are 3 types of achievements within this set:

<ol>
	<li> Glitches </li>
	<li> Damageless </li>
	<li> Endurance </li>
</ol>

These can all be collected within 2ish full playthroughs, split into 3 chapters. 


The general route for the playthroughs will be:

<ol>
	<li> Horse skip + early master sword (EMS) through tunicless Morpheel </li>
	<li> Full game deathless with 3 saves for the next run </li>
	<li> Remaining damageless dungeons + glitches. </li>
</ol>

Thanks to the folks at [zeldaspeedruns](https://www.zeldaspeedruns.com/tp), the glitches have a lot of documentation and tutorials that are actively updated to help you with setups and execution. 

As for the damageless routes, these are just the routes I used. I feel that they are consistent, but if you have a strategy that is more consistent, feel free to update the guide and add yourself as a contributor in the credits at the bottom! Just please remember, consistency for non-speedrunners is key and what the guide should be focused on.

Note that the achievement tables below do not look great if you are on mobile. There will be a collapsible section before each table that will be easier to scroll through if you are reading on your phone. Unfortunately, Markdown is pretty limited, so you will need to scroll past the tables once you are done with the section.

Let's get started with our first playthrough, up through Morhpeel.


## Abbreviations

Adopted from ZeldaSpeedRuns, that I will use within this guide:
- RS - rollstab(s)
- JA - jump attack(s)
- SA - spin attack(s)
- AS - autospin(s) (jump attack + spin attack)
- GS - great spin(s)
- BA - bomb arrow(s)
- LJA - Long Jump Attack
- EMS - Early Master Sword
- BiT - Back in Time

 - Stand at the back of the room to avoid all damage from the boss, only stepping forward as required to progress the fight.

## Chapter 2

| {% raachpic 415450, 469630, Hyrule's Sole Survivor (100) %} | Beat the game in a single session with 3 heart containers having collected all Fused Shadows and Twilight Fragments, and without dying or using the soft reset |

This achievement is pretty straight forward. And if you're doing this subset it should be no real issue for you. The only real tip I have to minimize the possibility of a mistake that can cost you hours of effort is to always have at least 1 fairy in a bottle with you.

You can get your first fairy directly in front of the boss room in Forest Temple. This will be inaccessible after your finish it until you get clawshot though.

The next fairy you can collect is in the big archer room in Goron Mines after you lower the gate (under where the chest that you need clawshot for is). This one is repeatable.

The next fairy is in a box floating in a puddle close to the wolf howling stone in Lake Hylia.

After this, complete the first 10 floors of Cave of Ordeals to unlock fairies in Ordon Spring so that you always have a quick way to refill if you need.

| {% raachpic 411128, 469638, Goron Mines No Damage %} | Complete the Goron Mines without taking damage |

- Go through the lava jets room as normal, LJA to skip the last one to avoid lava slugs.
- Grab the key, and make sure to wait for the platforms to spin once each before jumping on them, so that you don't jump on them as they start spinning.
- Run through the next room, wait for the lava jets while jumping on the rocks. Use the clip from the previous achievement to avoid needing to dodge more lava plumes.
- After getting the key, keep a distance from the slugs while going through the magnet sections.
- Kill all the moblins and make sure no keese are on you before stepping on both switches, otherwise they could initiate an attack during the cutscene that hits you after it ends.

The final damageless challenge, and certainly the one that takes the longest. There's a good chance you get it while working on The Fairy Hero achievement. Again, I highly recommend getting [infinite bombs and arrows](https://www.zeldaspeedruns.com/tp/misc/iza-1-skip-and-infinite-bomb-arrows#backup-to-kargarok-oob-clip) for this. Here's a [video of me completing it for a second time](https://youtu.be/Bfa2Mx_JxIk) while finishing off that achievement, and here was my general strategy for each floor. I copied and pasted the enemy list from [Zelda Dungeon](https://www.zeldadungeon.net/twilight-princess-walkthrough/cave-of-ordeals/).

A few things to keep in mind while doing this is that keese, rats, bubbles, and chus should not be underestimated, I was hit from behind by them several times. Kill as many from the ledge as you can before dropping down. I recommend not even getting close to any chus, just BA them from the ceiling before you jump down and from the ground after you jump down. 

Bubbles can float up to the ledge you are shooting from. If you see one rising up, stop shooting because there is a chance it pops up suddenly and makes you blow yourself up.

Bulblin archers are very dangerous, but they always go check on bombs, stand in the middle of the ledge and throw loose bombs by holding target and mashing the bomb button (do not press A to throw, or you may accidentally roll if there are 3 bombs loaded at once). Continue throwing until the door opens--your controller will rumble.

Freezards can be killed by baiting an attack, waiting for it to end, then closing the distance and hitting them twice with Ball and Chain. Do not underestimate the ice cubes they drop out--back up and BA them or Ball and Chain them if you have a decent distance.

Darknuts can all be fought 1 on 1, you just need to be patient. If you accidentally activate more than one, kite them around the arena only targeting one. Step forward to bait an attack and hit it 1-2 times until the armor comes off, then quickspin GS to break guard and quickspin GS once more to get a hit in. As long as you do not get greedy, you can get damageless with all 4 darknuts activated at once.

Since this strategy revolves around Bomb Arrows, be sure to always keep a good distance while shooting. If you think you are going to shoot the ledge you are standing on, reposition. If you think you are too close to an enemy, backflip to gain more distance, or bait an attack to increase the distance. You do not want to take damage from yourself.
